1. Introduction and Motivation for Revision

The original formulation of the W-R-Y Index (Ng, Hui Qin, & Lau, 2026a) provided a foundational framework for quantifying parasitic emotional load within a three-node interpersonal network. However, continued application of the governing equation across the 180-day observation period and its subsequent extension in Papers II–IV revealed several structural deficiencies that, while tolerable in isolation, compound under rigorous analytical use. This revised edition addresses these deficiencies while preserving the core typological framework and empirical findings of the original paper.

The principal deficiencies are: (i) the original equation produced a static, instantaneous snapshot with no time dependence, despite the study being explicitly longitudinal; (ii) the multiplicative treatment of the Gamma term implied that a fully functional Gamma could single-handedly neutralise the entire phenomenon, contradicting the Venn Topography's identification of the Delusion Zone (α ∩ β) as a standalone pathological region; (iii) the behavioural scales lacked a zero-anchor calibration point and interpolation protocol; and (iv) the Chaos Constant selection protocol did not address overlapping conditions.

2. The Revised Governing Equation

The W-R-Y Index is redefined as a cumulative integral over the observation duration T, measured in days:

IWRY(T) = ∫0T [ κ × (α(t) × β(t)) / N + γ(t) ] dt

where IWRY is the W-R-Y Index in Parasitic Emotional Joules (PEJ), T is the observation duration in days, α(t), β(t), and γ(t) are piecewise continuous behavioural scores on the 0–100 scale, κ is the condition-based Chaos Constant (0.5–1.5), and N = 100 is the Coupling Normalisation Constant.

2.1 Structural Rationale

The multiplicative coupling of α and β reflects a fundamental mechanical reality: over-investment is only catastrophic because it encounters dissonance, and dissonance is only parasitic because there exists investment to extract from. These pathologies amplify each other; multiplication captures this mutual escalation. The division by N = 100 (derived from the maximum single-axis score) rescales the αβ product from its raw range of 0–10,000 into the same order of magnitude as the γ term (0–100), ensuring both terms contribute meaningfully to the integrand.

The additive treatment of γ reflects Gamma's status as a parallel failure mode. Subject Gamma operates independently of the α–β feedback loop—running internal simulations in mechanical isolation, contributing frustration to the system in addition to the main coupled dynamic rather than because of it. At high αβ coupling, Gamma is a secondary irritant; at moderate coupling, Gamma can dominate the instantaneous load. Both outcomes are mechanically correct.

4. The Chaos Constant (κ): Selection Protocol

The Chaos Constant κ is determined by the dominant demographic character of the observer network at the time of measurement. When multiple conditions overlap (e.g., a mixed-gender group on Discord at 2 AM), the observer selects the single most influential condition under a dominant-condition rule. 5. Piecewise Continuous Time Evolution

The behavioural variables α(t), β(t), and γ(t) evolve as smooth continuous functions within behavioural phases, with permitted step discontinuities at identifiable trigger events. Human emotional states drift gradually (Alpha does not wake up one morning and jump from α = 50 to α = 80), but discrete shock events—such as Beta introducing Alpha as "just a friend" to a rival node—produce genuine overnight discontinuities. The mechanically honest model is therefore piecewise continuous: smooth curves within phases, with step changes at event boundaries.

The integral decomposes into a sum of phase integrals:

IWRY(T) = Σk ∫tktk+1 [ κ × (α(t) × β(t)) / N + γ(t) ] dt

where each phase k spans the interval between consecutive trigger events. Within each phase, the simplest admissible evolution model is linear drift:

α(t) = αk + mk(t − tk)

where αk is the post-trigger value at the start of phase k and mk is the drift rate (daily change in score). Higher-order models (exponential approach to saturation, logistic curves) are permitted but not required at this foundational stage. Each discontinuity is tagged to a specific observed event in the Trigger Event Catalogue, enabling per-phase PEJ contribution analysis.

8. Conclusion and Forward Compatibility

The revised governing equation corrects the structural deficiencies of the original formulation while preserving full backward compatibility with the Factorized Correction pipeline (Paper II), the origin mechanics and PID framework (Paper III), and the comparative H-O Standard Model (Paper IV). The integral formulation enables longitudinal tracking, per-phase contribution analysis, and direct identification of the trigger events that drove the system toward critical entanglement. The recalibrated hazard scale is grounded in stress-tested canonical scenarios rather than arbitrary threshold selection.

Future work will address the formal derivation of the Rationality Degradation Constant (Cdeg = 0.82), the integration of corrected vectors from Paper II into the revised governing equation, and the development of a κ composition function for overlapping observer-network conditions.
