Journal of Applied Social Mechanics & Interpersonal Kinematics

「学不会，根本学不会」

JASKM is an independent, open-access journal dedicated to the rigorous quantitative analysis of interpersonal phenomena that conventional social science has, for reasons both methodological and self-preserving, declined to study.

Founded in 2026, the journal emerged from a series of sustained naturalistic observations conducted across multiple McDonald's dining facilities in Melaka, Malaysia, where a localized three-node interpersonal stasis event — subsequently classified as the W-R-Y Phenomenon — was identified, quantified, and subjected to formal kinematic analysis.

The journal publishes original research articles, addenda, and comparative frameworks in the field of applied interpersonal kinematics. All papers undergo peer review by the full editorial board, which convenes at a corner booth.

Aims & Scope

Interpersonal Kinematics

The application of mechanical and thermodynamic principles to romantic and social dynamics. We model intent as kinetic energy, inaction as inertia, and rejection as a repulsive force vector — because someone had to.

Parasitic Load Analysis

Quantification of wasted emotional and capital resources within asymmetric interpersonal configurations, measured in Parasitic Emotional Joules (PEJ). The unit was invented because existing units of measurement were insufficiently depressing.

Comparative Framework

Establishment of normative baselines (the H-O Standard Model) against which pathological configurations can be formally evaluated. Current diagnostic sensitivity: 6,400:1 between anomalous and functional states.

Predictive Modeling

Stress-test simulations of interpersonal configurations under extreme environmental conditions, including but not limited to: post-midnight Discord voice channels, payday capital surges, and rival node intrusions.
